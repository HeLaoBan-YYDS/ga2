Are you looking to optimize your farming experience in *Grow a Garden 2*? ## What Does the Wheelbarrow Actually Do?

The primary function of the wheelbarrow in *Grow a Garden 2* revolves around player interaction, specifically dealing with other players on your server. Imagine this scenario: a rogue player attempts to pilfer your meticulously grown crops. This is where the wheelbarrow becomes your unexpected hero.

When equipped, you can run into another player's character model, and they will be scooped up and deposited inside the wheelbarrow. This allows you to pick them up and move them away from your precious garden, effectively protecting your crops from thieving hands. It's a rather amusing and effective way to deter unwanted visitors without resorting to more aggressive, less friendly tactics.

Beyond its anti-theft capabilities, the wheelbarrow also offers a subtle benefit for movement. You can place the wheelbarrow on the map and walk into it to drive it around. While operating it, your character's movement speed increases slightly. This can be beneficial for traversing your farm more quickly or even for making a swift getaway if you're the one attempting to "borrow" resources from another player's farm (though we certainly don't endorse such activities!). However, community reports indicate that the steering can be a bit challenging, so practicing its handling is recommended before relying on it in critical situations.

### Cost and Acquisition

The wheelbarrow can be acquired in one of two ways:

1.  **In-game Currency:** You can purchase the **Grow a Garden 2 wheelbarrow** for 500,000 Shekels. While this might seem like a substantial amount initially, player experience suggests that as your farm becomes more profitable, accumulating this sum becomes much more manageable.
2.  **Robux:** For those looking for instant gratification, the wheelbarrow can be bought for 129 Robux. However, general community consensus advises against spending Robux on items that can be earned through gameplay, especially when the Shekel cost is attainable through regular farming.
